MERVEX

MERVEX research group has been created to strengthening, at the "Instituto Español de Oceanografía" (IEO), the research related to the fishery resource assessment. The assessment is the process through which the consequences of different alternatives for management fishery resources are assessed (for example, catch or effort levels, closures, net sizes, etc). This process requires the combination of several research lines ranging from monitoring the activity of the fleet and model design, up to the evaluation of management strategies. Therefore, MERVEX participates in the following research lines.

  • Improvement of knowledge about the information of fleet activity.
  • Biology of exploited and non target marine species.
  • Dynamics of exploited populations.
  • Oceanographic surveys as a calibration tool for abundance in the models.
  • Development and application of predictive models that combine the dynamics of the stocks and the fleet activity calibrated using the results of the surveys.
  • Application of these models to the assessment of alternative management measures taking into account the uncertainty of the system.
  • Ecology of exploited populations taking into account environmental interactions, trophic relations, impact on the environment, etc and developing useful indicators for assessing the impact of fishing on it.
  • Development of models and indicators for the integrated assessment of marine ecosystems.
  • Development of models to quantify the social and economic impact of different fishery management measures, through the collaboration among the different organizations involved (industry, NGOs and politicians).
MERVEX participates in competitive projects of national and internacional scope financed by "Xunta de Galicia. Consellería de Economía, Emprego y Industria", "Axencia de Innovación (Gain)", "Instituto Español de Oceanografía", Ministry of Science and Innovation, State Research Agency, and European Social Fund. Members

MERVEX is a relatively new group (created in 2012) that supports the traditional advisory activity of the IEO through integrated fishery research. MERVEX integrates researchers from the Oceanographic Centers of Santander, A Coruña, Vigo and Cádiz to cover all the fisheries of interest to Spain in waters of the Northeast Atlantic in the ICES area. Although given the importance of fishing activity in Galicia, the Galician Oceanographic Centers are the ones that provide the large number of researchers.

  • New article "Life-history traits and environment shape small pelagic fish demography and responses to fishing and climate across European Atlantic seas" (17/05/2023)

    New article led by our colleague Jaime Otero on the responses of small pelagic fish (SPFs) to fisheries and climate change impacts. The results of this study highlight the importance of recruitment for the population growth of SPFs and illustrate how the contribution of recruitment varies among and within populations as a function of environmental conditions and life-history traits defining an ordination of SPFs along a slow-fast continuum with implications for population dynamics and the responses to endogenous and exogenous factors. Go to the article page, for more information.

  • New article "First Maximum Sustainable Yield advice for the Nephrops norvegicus stocks of the Northwest Iberian coast using stochastic Surplus Production model in Continuous Time (SPiCT)" (26/04/2023)
