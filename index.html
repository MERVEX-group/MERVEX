MERVEX

MERVEX research group has been created to strengthening, at the "Instituto Español de Oceanografía" (IEO), the research related to the fishery resource assessment. The assessment is the process through which the consequences of different alternatives for management fishery resources are assessed (for example, catch or effort levels, closures, net sizes, etc). This process requires the combination of several research lines ranging from monitoring the activity of the fleet and model design, up to the evaluation of management strategies. Therefore, MERVEX participates in the following research lines.

  • Improvement of knowledge about the information of fleet activity.
  • Biology of exploited and non target marine species.
  • Dynamics of exploited populations.
  • Oceanographic surveys as a calibration tool for abundance in the models.
  • Development and application of predictive models that combine the dynamics of the stocks and the fleet activity calibrated using the results of the surveys.
  • Application of these models to the assessment of alternative management measures taking into account the uncertainty of the system.
  • Ecology of exploited populations taking into account environmental interactions, trophic relations, impact on the environment, etc and developing useful indicators for assessing the impact of fishing on it.
  • Development of models and indicators for the integrated assessment of marine ecosystems.
  • Development of models to quantify the social and economic impact of different fishery management measures, through the collaboration among the different organizations involved (industry, NGOs and politicians).
MERVEX participates in competitive projects of national and internacional scope financed by "Xunta de Galicia. Consellería de Economía, Emprego y Industria", "Axencia de Innovación (Gain)", "Instituto Español de Oceanografía", Ministry of Science and Innovation, State Research Agency, and European Social Fund.. Members

MERVEX is a relatively new group (created in 2012) that supports the traditional advisory activity of the IEO through integrated fishery research. MERVEX integrates researchers from the Oceanographic Centers of Santander, A Coruña, Vigo and Cádiz to cover all the fisheries of interest to Spain in waters of the Northeast Atlantic in the ICES area. Although given the importance of fishing activity in Galicia, the Galician Oceanographic Centers are the ones that provide the large number of researchers.

  • Essential parameters for the Stocks Assessment: the length and the weight (15/10/2020)

    This week we talk about two other essential parameters for the Stocks Assessment: the length and the weight. The L-W relationship has 2 fundamental objectives:

    • 1. To predict the weight (unknown) from the length (known) of a fish. Very useful for calculating the biomass of a sample from the length frequency of that sample.
    • 2. To identify the relative condition of the population by comparing the L-W parameters estimated for a population “x” with:
      • (a) average parameters of the region
      • (b) estimated parameters from previous years
      • (c) other groups of fish.
    The b value (in W=aL^b) indicates how the growth of a species is:
    • b = 3 isometric (W and L increase proportionally)
    • b < 3 allometric negative (larger fish are larger than small ones)
    • b > 3 positive allometric (greater proportional gain in W than in L)
    In addition, to obtain the mean length and the number of individuals captured by age class, the so-called ALK (Age Length Key) are constructed. These keys transform the distribution of the catch by length class into an age distribution or ALD (Age Length Distribution). Or what is the same, by obtaining the parameters that define the growth of the species, we can describe the growth curve of each species and estimate the age that a fish of any length would have. The ALD allows knowing what fraction of the population has been exploited, which improves the evaluation of the species that seeks to achieve a sustainable exploitation of the resource.

  • The Second Workshop on Populating the RDBES data model (WKRDB-POP2) (03/06/2020)

    The RDBES will be extensively used by ICES member states, the EU Regional Coordination Groups, and ICES expert groups to store detailed commercial fisheries sample data and use it for estimation - therefore it is essential that national data submitters are familiar with the RDBES format and confident in correctly converting their national data to this format.The activities of this workshop will promote the development of a Regional Database and Estimation System, RDBES at ICES. This workshop will help countries to correctly convert their national data formats to the RDBES format. The RDBES when it is implemented works as a commercial fisheries database for the Baltic Sea, North Sea & Eastern Arctic, North Atlantic and Long Distance Fisheries Regional Coordination Groups (RCGs). The RDBES will also function as a database and estimation system for ICES Fisheries Advice. The development will concentrate on harmonisation, quality assuring, documentation, approved estimation methods and transparency. Consequently, these activities are considered to have a high priority. ICES will issue a data call in 2020 for 2019 samples, landings and effort data in the new RDBES format from selected stocks. The ideal conclusion is that at the end of this workshop each person attending has developed working scripts to extract the data that will be requested by the RDBES data call.
